1. In your own words, define what a game is.

A game is a structured activity where players follow rules, make decisions, and work toward a goal or outcome. Games are usually designed to be engaging, challenging, or entertaining, and they often involve competition, cooperation, strategy, or creativity.

2. What is Game Design?

Game design is the process of creating how a game works and how players experience it. It includes designing the rules, objectives, mechanics, challenges, rewards, story, and interactions that shape gameplay. Game designers focus on making games fun, balanced, and meaningful for players.

3. What is the MDA framework? What are its parts? Define each.

The MDA framework is a way of understanding and analyzing games. MDA stands for Mechanics, Dynamics, and Aesthetics. These three parts explain how games are built and how players experience them.
Mechanics – The rules, systems, and actions in the game. These include things like movement, scoring, health, turns, abilities, and win conditions. Mechanics are the foundation of gameplay.
Dynamics – The way the game behaves when players interact with the mechanics. Dynamics are the patterns and strategies that appear during gameplay, such as teamwork, competition, rushing, or careful planning.
Aesthetics – The emotional experience players have while playing. This includes feelings such as excitement, tension, fun, fear, accomplishment, or curiosity.


4. Use the MDA framework to analyze your modified game.

a. Mechanics

The modified rule is that when a player is tagged, they become frozen and cannot move. Frozen players can only be unfrozen if another teammate tags them. I also added a time limit of 5 minutes and safe zones where players can rest for only 10 seconds.

b. Dynamics

Compared to regular Tag, players now work together more because teammates must rescue frozen players. The safe zones create strategic movement because players must decide when to hide or keep running. The game becomes more teamwork-focused and tactical instead of just chasing.

c. Aesthetics

The modification changes the feelings of the game a lot. Regular Tag mainly feels fast and playful, but Freeze Tag Plus creates more tension and excitement because players worry about getting frozen. Players also feel teamwork, relief when rescued, and pressure when time is running out. The game feels more competitive and cooperative at the same time.
